# Editorial Support Guide

Editorial Support handles author correspondence, administrative tasks, and anything that keeps the editorial process moving smoothly without editorial decision authority. You work across all stages of the manuscript lifecycle.

## 1. Your dashboard

Navigate to `/journal/[acronym]/editorial/editorial-support`.

Your dashboard shows:
- **Open author correspondence** — queries and requests awaiting a response
- **Pending administrative tasks** — tasks assigned to the Editorial Support role
- **Recent decisions sent** — decision letters sent in the last 30 days (for follow-up queries)

## 3. Handling author queries

Common author queries and how to handle them:

**"When will I hear back about my submission?"**
Check the manuscript detail page for current status and the expected next milestone based on the workflow. Respond with the current status and an honest estimate — do not promise dates you cannot keep.

**"I made a mistake in my submission — can I correct it?"**
If the manuscript has not yet been passed to an editor, an Assistant Editor can unsubmit it for correction. If it is already with an editor or in review, the correction must be assessed for impact before any action.

**"I want to withdraw my submission"**
Withdrawals are possible at any stage before a final accept/reject decision. Record the withdrawal request and notify the handling editor.

**"I disagree with the decision"**
Formal appeals must be directed to the Editor-in-Chief. Acknowledge the query, explain the appeals process, and route the appeal letter to the EIC queue.

## 5. Post-decision correspondence

After a decision is sent, several follow-up tasks may fall to Editorial Support:

**Minor revision reminders**
If an author has not submitted their revision before the deadline, a reminder goes out automatically. If the deadline passes without a revision, the manuscript escalates to the EIC. You may need to field queries from authors requesting deadline extensions.

**Transfer coordination**
If a manuscript was rejected with a transfer offer and the author accepts, coordinate with the receiving journal's editorial office.

## 6. Escalating to editors

You do not make editorial decisions, but you can and should escalate to an editor when:

- An author raises a substantive complaint about the review process
- A query reveals a potential conflict of interest not previously disclosed
- A request requires editorial judgment (e.g. a significant post-submission correction)
- You are unsure how to respond to a sensitive query

Add a note to the manuscript history log when escalating, so the editor has full context.
